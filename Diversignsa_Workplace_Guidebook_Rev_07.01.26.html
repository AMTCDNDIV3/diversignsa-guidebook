Onboarding and Probationary Period

All new Stewards at Diversignsa begin with a 3-month probationary period. This period is designed to build trust relationships and integrate new team members into our Steward community.

Key Features:

  • Limited System Access: During probation, Stewards have limited access to software, applications, and internal systems. Full access is contingent upon clearance from a Human Connection (HC) Steward or Founding Steward.
  • Team Building Focus: The probationary period emphasizes relationship-building and cultural integration.
  • Week 1 Orientation: Comprehensive onboarding covering company policies, systems, and expectations.
  • Weeks 2–12 Integration: Gradual integration into team responsibilities with mentorship and support.
  • Performance Expectations: Clear performance standards are communicated and monitored throughout the probation period.

Probation Review Outcomes:

  • Successful Completion: Steward is fully integrated with unrestricted system access.
  • Extension: Probation may be extended if additional time is needed for integration or performance improvement.
  • Termination: Employment may be terminated if performance or cultural fit expectations are not met.

Termination During Probation:

Stewards terminated during the probationary period have limited recourse under standard employment law, as probationary employment is at-will. However, Diversignsa is committed to fair and transparent communication throughout this period.

Domestic Violence Support and Leave Policy

Diversignsa is committed to supporting Stewards who are experiencing domestic violence. We recognize that domestic violence can impact work performance and safety, and we provide comprehensive support and resources.

Purpose

To provide Stewards experiencing domestic violence with paid leave, flexible work arrangements, and access to support resources while maintaining confidentiality and ensuring workplace safety.

Definitions

Domestic Violence: Includes physical abuse, emotional abuse, sexual abuse, financial control, threats, stalking, or other controlling behaviors by a current or former intimate partner, family member, or household member.

Confidentiality

All disclosures of domestic violence are treated with strict confidentiality. Information is shared only on a need-to-know basis and only with the Steward's consent, except where required by law.

Paid Domestic Violence Leave

Eligible Stewards may take up to [5] paid days per calendar year for purposes related to domestic violence, including:

  • Seeking medical treatment or counseling
  • Obtaining legal assistance or court appearances
  • Securing safe housing
  • Attending support group meetings
  • Addressing safety concerns

Immediate Request Requirement

Stewards must request paid domestic violence leave immediately after the domestic violence incident has occurred. Requests submitted significantly after the incident may be subject to review and approval.

Flexible Work Arrangements

Stewards may request flexible work schedules, remote work options, or temporary schedule changes to address safety concerns or attend to domestic violence-related matters.

Safety Measures

Diversignsa will work with affected Stewards to implement reasonable safety measures, which may include:

  • Workplace security assessments
  • Modified work locations or schedules
  • Screening of workplace visitors
  • Emergency contact protocols

Non-Retaliation

Diversignsa strictly prohibits retaliation against any Steward for requesting or using domestic violence leave, disclosing domestic violence, or participating in related proceedings.

Resources and Support

Diversignsa provides access to the following resources:

  • Abused Deaf Women Advocacy Service (ADWAS): 1-855-500-3323 (TTY/Video Relay available) | Website: www.adwas.org
  • National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233 (TTY: 1-800-787-3224)
  • Steward Assistance Program (EAP): Confidential counseling and support services
  • Local domestic violence shelters and legal aid organizations
